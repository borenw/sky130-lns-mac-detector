Multiplier vs. K=1 Log/LNS Spike Detector

Two RTL designs of the same function spike = (A·B + C·D) > Vth — an exact-multiplier baseline and a multiplier-free log-domain (LNS, K=1) variant — synthesized and compared on the sky130 HD standard-cell library.

Dropping the two multipliers for the K=1 log detector cuts area 28% and estimated power 27%, at a 4.94% accuracy cost vs. exact math.

K=1 accuracy cost — disagreement vs exact

Over the full 32⁴ input space × each threshold (≈12.6 M evaluations). Design 2's RTL is bit-exact to its K=1 model; a disagreement with exact math is the approximation cost, not a bug. Overall = 4.94%, peaking at mid-range thresholds and vanishing at the extremes.
